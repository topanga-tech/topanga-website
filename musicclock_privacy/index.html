Music Clock for Spotify Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 13, 2025

Music Clock for Spotify lets you choose songs, playlists, or artists from Spotify and set alarms that start with your selected music. This Privacy Policy explains how Music Clock collects, uses, and protects your information when you use the app.

Information Collection and Use

To play music from Spotify at your scheduled alarms, Music Clock uses Spotify's authorization (OAuth) to request only the minimum permissions required to control playback on your devices. We do not collect or store your Spotify password.

  • We may receive a limited-access token from Spotify to control playback on your devices.
  • Alarm schedules and settings are stored locally on your device.
  • We do not store your playlists, listening history, or personal profile data on our servers.
  • We may collect anonymous usage analytics to improve the app experience.

Data Storage and Security

Access tokens are stored securely on your device and are not shared with third parties. We implement appropriate security measures to protect data handled by the app.

Third-Party Services

Music Clock integrates with Spotify to enable playback. Spotify handles your account information under its own policies. We do not share user-provided data with third parties.

Children's Privacy

Music Clock is not intended for users under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we discover such data, we will delete it immediately.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Any changes will be posted on this page, and continued use of Music Clock signifies your acceptance of the updated policy.
